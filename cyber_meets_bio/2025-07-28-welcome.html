⚙️ Bioelectric Basics

All cells maintain a resting membrane potential. Neurons and muscle cells modulate this actively, but *all* membranes rely on ionic gradients and structural polarization:

Typical neuron: ~ -70mV resting potential
Membrane capacitance: ~1 µF/cm²

This makes biological tissue both conductive *and* reactive to external EM influences — not unlike tuned circuits.

📐 Known EM Field Effects

  • Weak pulsed fields (Hz–kHz) have been shown to modulate calcium ion release
  • Static magnetic fields influence proton alignment (see: MRI principles)
  • Millimeter wave exposure linked to altered gene expression (with debate)

One emerging idea is that cells may **naturally oscillate** at characteristic frequencies depending on their type and state — which opens the door to resonance-based tuning or interference.

🔍 Analogies from Electronics

If you treat cells as RLC circuits (resistor–inductor–capacitor), resonance theory gives a framework for potential interaction. Each cellular structure could act like a **micro-antenna** tuned to specific frequencies based on its size, ionic composition, and membrane properties.

Resonant frequency (simplified): f = 1 / (2π√(LC))
Where:
  L = inductance (circulating ion current loops?)
  C = capacitance of membrane

Whether this holds in living systems is still under debate — but it's a useful model.
